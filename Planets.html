8 Planètes à Explorer
Conditions Climatiques Uniques
Caractéristiques Fascinantes

Mercure

Diameter: ~4,880 km

Distance from the Sun: ~58 million km

Temperature: -173 °C to +427 °C

Mercury is the smallest and closest planet to the Sun, with no atmosphere to regulate temperature.

Vénus

Diameter: ~12,104 km

Distance from the Sun: ~108 million km

Average Temperature: 462 °C

Venus is the hottest planet, shrouded in thick, toxic clouds that trap heat with a strong greenhouse effect.

Terre

Diameter: ~12,742 km

Distance from the Sun: ~150 million km

Average Temperature: 15 °C

Earth is the only known planet to support life, with liquid water, a protective atmosphere, and diverse ecosystems.

Jupiter

Diameter: ~139,820 km

Distance from the Sun: ~778 million km

Temperature: -145 °C

Jupiter is the largest planet, known for its Great Red Spot storm and powerful magnetic field.

Mars

Diameter: ~6,779 km

Distance from the Sun: ~228 million km

Average Temperature: -60 °C

Mars, the red planet, has two moons and features massive volcanoes and ancient riverbeds.

Neptune

Diameter: ~49,244 km

Distance from the Sun: ~4.5 billion km

Temperature: -214 °C

Neptune is the windiest planet in the solar system, with storms and deep blue methane clouds.

Uranus

Diameter: ~50,724 km

Distance from the Sun: ~2.9 billion km

Temperature: -224 °C

Uranus is an ice giant that spins on its side, giving it unique seasons lasting over 20 years.

Saturn

Diameter: ~116,460 km

Distance from the Sun: ~1.4 billion km

Temperature: -178 °C

Saturn is famous for its majestic rings and dozens of moons, including the large moon Titan.
